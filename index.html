Urban Trees Help Platform

Leveraging Technology and Community for Effective Green Space Preservation

Trees in cities are crucial but vulnerable

City trees and green spaces improve the city environment by

  • Reducing local temperatures
  • Fighting air & sound pollution
  • Enhancing the visual quality of the city environment

However, they face critical threats due to

  • Rapid climate change and extreme weather
  • Urban development and increasing technological pressure
  • Insufficient resources and outdated management practices

Local governments need our help

Many different municipalities encounter similar challenges

  • Limited budgets and time for green planning and maintenance
  • Understaffing and low professional level of tree care specialists
  • Difficulty in engaging communities effectively
  • Underutilization of modern and advanced technologies (e.g., AI, Social Media

Our IT-backed project can help

These challenges could be solved with

  • Adoption of advanced technologies for efficient management
  • Enhanced community engagement and involvement
  • Utilizing data to improve decision-making and monitoring

The Plan

To clearly outline our goals and the steps we’re taking, we’ve structured our project using the following categories

Resources
Actions

Resources

We currently have

  • ekb-trees-help.ru: A functioning platform for urban green space management
  • An unfinished computer vision project for fast tree inventory
  • 3 volunteer developers and 1 local volunteer community

What we need:

  • Additional funding for accelerated software development
  • Partnerships with municipalities and local volunteer groups
  • Engagement with the scientific community for data analysis and insights

Actions

IT Platform Development Plan

  • Optimize the codebase and releasing it as open source
  • Add new map layers for broader regional coverage
  • Implement computer vision for automatic tree inventory
  • Integrate data visualization and analysis tools
  • Add an issue tracker subsystem for maintenance management

Community and Municipality Engagement

  • Secure interest from 3 municipalities to adopt the system
  • Partner with at least 3 volunteer groups focused on green space protection
  • Conduct comprehensive green space inventories
  • Foster collaboration between municipalities and volunteers for better trees care using the IT platform

Scientific Community Collaboration

  • Engage 3-5 researchers to develop better approaches for urban climate challenges
  • Collaborate with landscaping experts to provide actionable insights to municipalities

Outputs

Expected outputs include

  • An open-source tool for green space inventory and maintenance
  • A SaaS platform for commercial users
  • Mapped urban green spaces with detailed tree condition information
  • Established collaborations between municipalities and local communities

Outcomes

Expected outcomes of the project

  • Enhanced care and accuracy in urban tree inventory and health monitoring
  • Increased community involvement and stronger environmental connections
  • Empowered volunteers globally to protect and manage green spaces

Impact

The long-term impact includes

  • Mitigation of urban heat extremes and other climate challenges
  • Transformation in planning and maintaining green spaces with new technologies and community engagement
